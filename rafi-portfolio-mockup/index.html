Rafi James
About Me.

I'm a design-minded, Game Designer that has been a part of the Game indsutry for 8+ years. Join me on a journey through the art I’ve done to the games I’ve created.



I’m Rafi James and I love games, from board games to videogames. Ever since I was a kid I’ve been into games, most of my childhood memories are of playing games. Also, I have a passion for tech and my curiosity about how things work got me into taking everything around me apart. Eventually, I started disassembling the tech that I used and modifying it to work better for me. My love of games and modifying tech lead me down the road of customizing and creating my own games.

What I do.
Game designer

I pursued my Bachelor’s Degree in Game Design from Full Sail University where I was recognized as one of the top students in my program and salutatorian of my graduating class. Upon graduation, I launched into my career as a game designer starting at Fireshark, an indie game studio based in my hometown. As a primary designer at Fireshark, I gained professional experience in building design docs, prototyping, level design and content development. Through this experience, I’ve successfully shipped 3 video games spanning across multiple genres including, battle arena, racing and fighting.

Game Tester

My game testing experience spans across many facets in the game industry from AAA to indie and volunteering as an alpha/beta tester. In the AAA arena I’ve worked at Nintendo of America as a tester for Fire Emblem: Three Houses, Legend of Zelda: Link’s Awakening and Smash Bros DLC. At Microsoft I was involved in game hardware testing such as stress and compatibility testing the Xbox Series X and S.

Developer

I’ve taken initiative in learning basics of programming across multiple languages such as C#, JavaScript and Python. I’ve applied the concepts I’ve learned in hobby projects by using such devices as a Raspberry Pi and Arduino. In a professional setting at Fireshark Studios I assisted developers with modifying code within Unity.

QA Engineer

As a QA Engineer I’ve worked alongside designers and developers to prototype and test game features before being implemented in-game. Oftentimes my feedback about a feature results in an open conversation with designers and developers about what could be improved or different features that could be added to enhance the players’ experience.

Professional Experience.

Super Smash Bros. Ultimate

I worked as a system debug tester for the 5th and final downloadable character, Byleth, from the Fighters Pass Vol. 1. For this project we mainly focused on issues that could happen with the character, Byleth, and his/her stage. Also, I was personally in charge of performing a system check for each new build we received, to ensure the system was stable and all character behaved correctly.

Legend of Zelda: Link’s Awakening

A remake of the original gameboy game, released on the Nintendo Switch. I worked as a system debug tester, looking for any kind of issue that could hinder gameplay or cause the system to crash.

Fire Emblem: Three Houses

The first entry of the Fire Emblem series on the Nintendo Switch. I was a game tester that specialized in text check. This included searching for spelling and grammatical errors, as well as context and story inconsistencies.

Rift Heroes

Rift Heroes is a fighting game that was released on the Fireshark Theater version 2. I was in charge of finalizing the all the characters, their movesets, and balancing. Once the game was near release, I set up play test sessions, gathered data and made any changes that were needed.

Wreck3d

This is second game to be released on the Fireshark Theater. This is a racing game that allowed the players to race on the floor and walls and had multiple game modes. I selected all the vehicles and tuned their stats. I assisted in finalizing the different game modes and stages.

Mech Mayhem

The flagship game that was released on the Fireshark Theater. This is a battle arena game that included 7 different mechs with 3 different game styles. I helped in balancing the mechs and overall quality assurance of the game.

MARKET MAYHEM
Overview
The player is shopping to collect everything on their list. While doing so they must deal with the horrible handling of the shopping cart and annoying customers that get in the way. This takes place in a top-down view. However, if the player runs into another customer the game's view and game type changes to a 2D fighting game. In this mode the player is taking on the customer they just ran into in a simplistic fighting game. The goal for each stage is to complete your shopping list within a certain time limit. Each subsequent stage will create a new list with more items. After approx 5 stages, a new level layout will be presented. Such as, the first 5 stages the player is in a mini mart and the next level would introduce a grocery store/market. The final level could be a super store.
Goal
Gather all the items on the shopping list within the time limit.
Top-down Shopping
Zelda dungeon-esque view style top-down. Shopping list on the left side of the screen, a quarter of the screen (maybe less). The rest of the screen is dedicated to the shop. The basic layout of the shop will remain the same for each level, the items that need to be collected change from stage to stage. The player can move around and have a button to dash. (Change) Make the Shopping list translucent and let the player toggle it with a push of a button.
2D Fighting
Side view. HUD will have a player avatar’s face and hearts for hit points. One heart for each HP. The player can move left, right and duck. Player actions are attack, block and throw. Throw can only be used if the player has picked up items. If the player has an item the items will appear as small icons under the avatar’s face and hearts. Whichever items are thrown need to be recollected, once back in shopping mode.

Falling for Putin
About
This game was created at a game jam. We were a 3 person team and had less than 3 days to go from an idea to a playable game. The theme for this game jam was Useless Super Powers. My team came up with the idea of flying downwards but at terminal velocity for a human being. I contributed with game design, which consisted of taking all of our ideas and making them viable game mechanics. Also, I created all of the game art. The art we made using Photoshop and the game was built using Unity. We were one of the top contenders but ended up winning second place.

7 Minutes to ______ the World
About
This was created at the ICT 2019 summer game jam. I was one of the artist, as well as a game designer. The theme was Start from Nothing, so we created a clicker game. As per typical clicker games the player starts off with nothing. The player can either choose to be evil or good by choosing to click on doing a good or evil action. As suggested in the title of the game, you have 7 minutes to either save or rule the world. As the game progresses the imagery changes based off if the player is choosing a righteous or wicked path. We won 5th place and our game was the crowd favorite.

About
This is a game I created using Perlenspiel. Perlenspiel is a cloud-based HTML5 engine that uses Javascript as the programming language. It uses beads in a max of 32×32 grid.
